Hello! Thanks for agreeing to participate in our study. For this experiment, you will be competing against other Turkers in a classic turn-based game, Connect-Four



Overview

The object of the game is to drop pieces such that you connect four adjacent pieces of your color -- diagonally, vertically, or horizontally -- before your oponent can do the same. We've added the additional caveat of short turn time limits to spice things up. After {{ layout_conf.turn_timeout }} seconds, a random action will be played on your turn. Move quickly! Bonus Pay will be awarded based on the number of games you win, so it's best to play deliberately and precisely



Graphics

Below is a labeled screenshot of the game graphics: Of note:

  • Turn Timer: Indicates how many seconds are remaining in your (or your opponent's) turn
  • Turn Indicator: Text indicating whether it is your or your opponent's turn
  • Game Board: Current state of the playing board, filled with your and your opponent's pieces
  • Column Labels: Columns are numbered 1-7. Pressing the corresponding number key on your keyboard drops a piece in the corresponding column



    • Controls

    Columns are labeled 1-9. On your turn, you drop a piece in a column by pressing the corresponding number on your keyboard. The number keypad works as well Let's see a quick example. Suppose you start with the board below. You can tell from the graphics that it's your turn to play. You press the "4" key. Your token (blue) is dropped in column four and falls to the lowest available slot. The board now looks as shown below: Note: If you try to play in a column that is full, nothing will happen. You can only play in open columns!



    Winning

    There are three ways to win, four in a row vertically, horizontally, or diagonally. An example of each case is pictured below:

    If no empty slots remain and neither player has acheived four in a row, the game results in a draw.

    Remember, there is a bonus pay determined by the number of games you win, so it's best to have a strategy and play competetively. Good luck!
